### Who We Are
Toronto Academy of English, Mathematics and Coding, or simply EMC, is a private high school, accredited by the Ontario Ministry of Education, providing high-quality education for students from grades 9-12 based on the official Ontario Secondary School Curriculum. We are also an IB WORLD SCHOOL for the DIPLOMA PROGRAMME.

---

### Our Story
EMCS was founded with a mission to bring world-class Canadian education to students around the globe. We believe that geography should never be a barrier to quality education.

### Our Mission
To provide flexible, high-quality Ontario curriculum education that empowers students to achieve their academic goals and gain admission to top universities worldwide.

Toronto Academy of EMC strives to inspire and equip our students with the necessary mindset and skills to become independent lifelong learners, fully dedicated to making tangible and positive contributions to their communities and beyond.

**Subtitle:** What you need to graduate with an Ontario Secondary School Diploma

---

### Requirements Overview
- **Total Credits Required:** 30
- **Compulsory Credits:** 18
- **Optional Credits:** 12
- **Literacy Test (OSSLT):** Required
- **Community Hours:** 40

---

### 18 Compulsory Credits
**Subtitle:** Required subjects for all OSSD graduates

| Subject | Credits | Notes |
|---------|---------|-------|
| English | 4 | 1 credit per grade (9, 10, 11, 12) |
| Mathematics | 3 | At least 1 in Grade 11 or 12 |
| Science | 2 | |
| Canadian History | 1 | Grade 10 |
| Canadian Geography | 1 | Grade 9 |
| Arts | 1 | Music, Art, Drama, or Dance |
| Health & Physical Education | 1 | |
| French as a Second Language | 1 | |
| Career Studies | 0.5 | |
| Civics | 0.5 | |
| Group 1, 2, or 3 | 3 | Additional from specified groups |

---

### Additional Requirements
**Subtitle:** Beyond credits, you must also complete:

#### Ontario Secondary School Literacy Test (OSSLT)
Must successfully complete the OSSLT or the Ontario Secondary School Literacy Course (OSSLC).

#### 40 Hours Community Involvement
Complete 40 hours of community service before graduation.

#### Online Learning Requirement
Minimum of 2 online learning credits (fulfilled through EMCS courses).

## FAQ

### Header
**Title:** Frequently Asked Questions

**Subtitle:** Find answers to common questions about our online school

---

### Questions & Answers

**Q: Is EMCS accredited?**
A: Yes, EMCS is a Ministry-inspected private school authorized to grant OSSD credits.

**Q: When can I start?**
A: We have open enrollment year-round. You can start any course immediately after registration.

**Q: How long do I have to complete a course?**
A: You have 12 months from enrollment to complete each course, though most students finish in 3-4 months.

**Q: Are there final exams?**
A: Yes, all credit courses require a proctored final exam worth 30% of your grade.

**Q: How do I get support?**
A: Teachers respond to questions within 48 hours. We also have guidance counselors for university applications.

**Q: What courses do you offer?**
A: We offer 170+ courses across all subjects for Grades 9-12, including English, Mathematics, Sciences, Business, and more. All courses follow the Ontario curriculum.

**Q: Can I transfer credits from another school?**
A: Yes! We accept transfer credits from Ontario and other Canadian schools. International students can also have their transcripts evaluated for credit equivalency.

**Q: How do final exams work?**
A: Final exams are worth 30% of your grade and must be proctored. You can write your exam online with a remote proctor, or at an approved testing center.

**Q: Do you help with university applications?**
A: Absolutely! Our guidance counselors help students with OUAC and OCAS applications, personal statements, and scholarship applications for Canadian universities.

**Q: What technology do I need?**
A: You need a computer or tablet with internet access, a webcam for proctored exams, and basic software like a web browser and PDF reader.

## Course Catalog

### Grade 12 Courses (University Preparation)

#### ENG4U - Grade 12 English
**Type:** University | **Prerequisite:** ENG3U, Grade 11 English

Grade 12 University English consolidates literacy and communication along with the creative and critical thinking skills necessary for success in both academic and daily life.

During the ENG4U course, students analyse a range of challenging literary, informational, and graphic texts from various periods, countries, and cultures. They will also create oral, written, and media texts in a variety of forms.

**Curriculum Focus:**
- Oral communication
- Reading and literature studies
- Writing
- Media studies

**Units:**
1. Unit 1: Novel Study (The Wars or Life of Pi) - 25 hours
2. Unit 2: Drama (Hamlet or Othello) - 25 hours
3. Unit 3: Poetry and Short Stories - 20 hours
4. Unit 4: Media Studies - 15 hours
5. Unit 5: Independent Study Unit (ISU) - 25 hours

**Evaluation:** 70% term work, 30% final exam

**FAQs:**
- What is ENG4U? ENG4U is a Grade 12 English high school course at a University preparation level. ENG4U is a required prerequisite course for most university programs.
- What do you Learn in University Preparation Grade 12 English (ENG4U)? During Grade 12 English, students learn the literacy communication and creative thinking skills necessary for success in academic and daily life.
- What are 4U courses? 4U refers to the Grade level of the courses and the pathway. 4 means it is a Grade 12 English in Ontario course, and U means the course is intended to prepare students for university.
- What is the Prerequisite Course for ENG4U? Prerequisite: ENG3U, Grade 11 English or NBE3U, English: Understanding Contemporary First Nations, Métis, and Inuit Voices.
- How Long Does it take to Complete the ENG4U Online Course? At EMCS you can complete an online high school credit courses as quickly as 4 weeks, or take as long as 12 months.
- Will my Marks be Sent Directly to OUAC or OCAS? Yes, we can send the marks for your online courses directly to OUAC, OCAS, your home, and to your day school.

#### MHF4U - Advanced Functions
**Type:** University | **Prerequisite:** MCR3U or MCT4C

Grade 12 Advanced Functions extends students experience with functions. Students will investigate the properties of polynomial, rational, logarithmic, and trigonometric functions; develop techniques for combining functions; broaden their understanding of rates of change; and develop facility in applying these concepts and skills.

**Curriculum Focus:**
- Exponential and Logarithmic Functions
- Trigonometric Functions
- Polynomial and Rational Functions
- Characteristics of Functions

**Units:**
1. Unit 1: Polynomial Functions - 20 hours
2. Unit 2: Polynomial Equations and Inequalities - 15 hours
3. Unit 3: Rational Functions - 15 hours
4. Unit 4: Trigonometry - 25 hours
5. Unit 5: Trigonometric Functions - 15 hours
6. Unit 6: Exponential and Logarithmic Functions - 20 hours

**Evaluation:** 70% term work, 30% final exam

---

#### SBI4U - Grade 12 Biology
**Type:** University | **Prerequisite:** SBI3U

Grade 12 Biology allows students an in-depth look at the processes occurring in the biological systems all around them – and even inside their own bodies! Students will study theory and conduct investigations in the areas of biochemistry, metabolic processes, molecular genetics, homeostasis, and population dynamics.

**Curriculum Focus:**
- Biochemistry
- Metabolic processes
- Molecular genetics
- Homeostasis
- Population dynamics

**Units:**
1. Unit 1: Biochemistry - 20 hours
2. Unit 2: Metabolic Processes - 25 hours
3. Unit 3: Molecular Genetics - 25 hours
4. Unit 4: Homeostasis - 20 hours
5. Unit 5: Population Dynamics - 20 hours

**Evaluation:** 70% term work, 30% final exam
